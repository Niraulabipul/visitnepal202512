About Nepal

Nepal is a landlocked nation situated in South Asia, bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west. Famous for its breathtaking natural beauty, it is home to Mount Everest, the highest mountain in the world, and offers a diverse landscape with the flat Terai plains, lush hills, and towering peaks. Kathmandu, the capital and largest city, along with other notable cities like Pokhara and Lalitpur, is a hub of cultural and historical significance. The country boasts a rich cultural tradition, with Hinduism being the dominant religion, followed by Buddhism, and smaller Muslim and Christian communities. Major festivals such as Dashain, Tihar, and Buddha Jayanti reflect the country's vibrant traditions. Nepal's history dates back to ancient times, and it was a monarchy until 2008, when it became a federal democratic republic. Though it has faced political instability, Nepal is making strides toward greater stability. Tourism is a major contributor to the economy, particularly for trekking, mountaineering, and spiritual tourism, while agriculture and remittances from Nepali workers abroad also play a key role. Nepali is the official language, though over 120 other languages are spoken across the country, highlighting its ethnic diversity. The climate varies from tropical in the lowlands to alpine in the high mountains, supporting diverse ecosystems and agricultural practices.
